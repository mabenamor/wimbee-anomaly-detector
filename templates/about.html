At Wimbee, we have designed a hybrid anomaly detection engine that combines two complementary machine learning models:

  • One-Class SVM: A model that learns the boundary of normal transactions and flags deviations. It works well in high-dimensional space and captures non-linear structures.
  • Custom DBSCAN-like Method: Instead of using traditional DBSCAN which is sensitive to hyperparameters, we engineered a hand-crafted density-based method using nearest neighbors to better detect outliers in Murex financial data.

Together, these two methods vote on each transaction. If at least two out of three methods agree (including an optional third model), the trade is flagged as suspicious.

ðŸš« Why not Isolation Forest?

We evaluated Isolation Forest extensively. While it performs well in many generic anomaly detection tasks, we found it underperformed on Murex-like financial data:

  • It frequently labeled normal transactions as anomalies.
  • It lacked sensitivity to subtle but important deviations specific to market logic.

Conclusion: We chose to remove Isolation Forest from our final voting system to ensure better precision and trust in the predictions.
